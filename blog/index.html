What to do when the... Project Failed

IT Chalenges of working for a growing company.

by John Bull

Posted 10/27/15

It’s a good problem to have in the business world. Rapid growth and expansion in our current business has the IT team that I am a part of scrambling to keep up. With 50% growth over the last 5 years the little company we support is growing in leaps and bounds. The struggles we go through range from deploying hardware at the rate of new hires, to growing our virtual server infrastructure to handle double the amount of servers that we had 6 months ago. The challenges we are facing have us asking some tough questions. How can we better manage our projects so that communication with our stakeholders, our team and our outside support? The goal being to have a greater degree of confidence in the success of our projects going in. Do we need to have a higher tier of support as we make headway into a more and more Enterprise level infrastructure? How can we analyze our effectiveness as a team? I’m sure there will be much more as we move forward. These questions are just some that we are looking at as we make transitions and grow as a company. We all should ask ourselves from time to time, “How can we improve the situation, workflow, or process”, and we are doing that more and more often.
After the recent crash and burn of a large networking/virtualization project we were all left with a sour taste in our mouths.
