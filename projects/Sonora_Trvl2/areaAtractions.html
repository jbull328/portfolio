Attractions

Iconic Setting

The picturesque landscape offers photographers and artists unforgettable opportunities to capture iconic images. One of the most photographed churches is located right in the heart of Sonora. St. James Church or The Red Church was built in 1860 and one of the foothills most recognizable landmarks.

Columbia State Park

A Living History

This gold rush era town is one of the best places to learn about California's rich history. Built in the 1850's boom, the park features a working blacksmith, candy factory, history exhibits, gold panning and much more. One of the most exciting exhibits at Columbia is the Stage coach. Filled with colorful characters, a ride on the stagecoach is guaranteed to entertain young and old alike.

The Great Outdoors

Sonora is a gateway to the highway 108 section of the Sierra Nevada's. With an elevation ranging from 3000 ft to 9000 ft the area is chalk full of hidden lakes, campgrounds hiking and quiet getaway spots for your next mountain jaunt. Visit Pinecrest lake in the summer to beat the heat, or learn to ski at Dodge Ridge in the winter. You can't go wrong with a mountain adventure in this area. Just be sure to pack extra food, water, and warm clothing as the weather can change at any time in the higher altitudes.

GoldRush

Foothills Gold

Weather relaxing or trying your hand at gold panning, there is always something new in this area to experience. Area wines, great local restaurants, and hidden gems await in this gold rush town.
